UPI Fraud Case Study

UPI fraud is a common cybercrime where attackers trick users into sharing sensitive information. The victim usually receives a call or message pretending to be from a bank or service provider. The attacker may ask for an OTP or send a fake payment request. Once the victim shares the OTP or approves the request, money is deducted instantly from their account.

This type of fraud mainly targets students, elderly people, and individuals who are not aware of online scams. The consequences include financial loss, stress, and loss of trust in digital systems.

UPI fraud is increasing rapidly due to the rise in digital payments. Therefore, awareness and careful usage of online payment systems are very important to prevent such crimes.
